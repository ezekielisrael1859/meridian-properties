Answers You

Title verification is a critical step in any Nigerian property transaction. At Meridian, we conduct a comprehensive search at the relevant land registry — confirming Certificate of Occupancy (C of O) status, checking for encumbrances, and engaging licensed surveyors and property lawyers before any client commitment is made. We handle this entirely on your behalf.

Beyond the agreed purchase price, buyers typically account for: agency fees (usually 5–10% of property value), legal fees (1–5%), stamp duty, and registration charges. In some cases, development levies or estate management fees apply. We provide a full cost breakdown before any commitment so there are no surprises.

Yes. Foreign nationals can legally purchase property in Nigeria, subject to certain regulatory approvals. Our legal partners have significant experience advising diaspora and international buyers, and we guide you through every requirement — from the Land Use Act provisions to any applicable foreign exchange considerations.

A standard luxury property transaction in Abuja or Lagos takes between 4 and 12 weeks from offer acceptance to completion, depending on the complexity of title documentation, financing arrangements, and the responsiveness of both parties. Meridian actively manages the timeline and keeps you informed at every stage.
